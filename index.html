Cyber Attack Incident Response Checklist
This form provides a structured checklist to document and track key actions taken during a cyber attack incident response. It ensures that each phase of the response process is completed and properly recorded.
Incident Discovery and Confirmation
No. Process Team Member Time Log
1 How did the team first learn of the attack (security researcher, partner, employee, customer, auditor, internal security alert, etc.). Examine audit logs and security applications for unusual or suspicious account behavior or activities that indicate a potential attack, and confirm attack has transpired.
2 Describe potential attackers, including capabilities, behaviors, and motivations that are known or expected.
3 Identify access point and source of attack (endpoint, application, malware downloaded, etc.) and responsible party.
4 Prepare an incident timeline to keep an ongoing record of when the attack occurred and subsequent milestones in analysis and response.
5 Check for signatures, IP address ranges, files hashes, processes, executables names, URLs, and domain names of known malicious websites in your applications.
6 Assess the scope of the damage and the risk to systems and privileged accounts upon discovery. Check to see if any privileged accounts have been used recently, whether any passwords have been changed, and what applications have been executed.
7 Examine your list of information assets to identify which assets may have been compromised. Take note of the assets’ integrity as well as the evidence gathered.
8 Diagram the path of the incident/attack to provide an “at-a-glance” view from the initial breach to escalation and movement tracked across the network.
9 Collect meeting notes in a central repository to use in preparing communications with stakeholders.
10 Inform employees regarding discovery. Analyze incident Indicators of Compromise (IOCs) with threat intelligence tools
11 Potentially share information externally about breach discovery. To improve your chances of catching the attacker, you may choose to hold communications during this phase until the breach has been contained. If so, make sure that it complies with your legal requirements.
Incident Containment and Continuity
No. Process Team Member Time Log
1 Allow the technical and security team to utilize temporary privileged accounts to quickly access and monitor systems.
2 Evidence must be safeguarded. Before performing any actions that may affect data integrity on the original media, make a backup of any compromised systems as soon as possible.
3 Force multi-factor authentication or peer review to ensure privileges are being used appropriately.
4 Change passwords for all users, service, application, and network accounts.
5 To prevent malicious malware from being distributed by the attacker, increase the sensitivity of application security controls (allowing, denying, and restricting).
6 Remove systems from production or take systems offline if needed.
7 Inform employees regarding breach containment.
8 Analyze, record, and confirm any instances of potential data exfiltration occurrences across the network.
9 Potentially share information externally regarding breach containment (website updates, emails, social media posts, tech support bulletins, etc.).
Eradication
No. Process Team Member Time Log
1 Close firewall ports and network connections. Test devices and applications to be sure any malicious code is removed.
2 Compare data before and after the incident to ensure systems are reset properly.
3 Inform employees regarding eradication.
4 Potentially share information externally regarding eradication (website updates, emails, social media posts, tech support bulletins, etc.).
Recovery
No. Process Team Member Time Log
1 Download and apply security patches.
2 Close network access and reset passwords.
3 Conduct vulnerability analysis
4 Return any systems that were taken offline to production.
5 Inform employees regarding recovery.
6 Share information externally regarding recovery (website updates, emails, social media posts, tech support bulletins, etc.).
Lessons Learned
No. Process Team Member Time Log
1 Review forensic evidence collected.
2 Assess incident cost.
3 Write an Executive Summary of the incident.
4 Report to the executive team and auditors if required
5 Implement additional training for everyone involved in incident response and all employees.
6 Update incident response plan.
7 Inform employees regarding lessons learned, additional training, etc.
8 Potentially share information externally (website updates, emails, social media posts, tech support bulletins, etc.).
